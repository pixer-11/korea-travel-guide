> Visa and entry rules change frequently and depend on your nationality. This is general information, not immigration advice — always confirm with an official government source before you book.

## Visa-free, visa on arrival, or apply ahead?

Most countries fall into three buckets for a given passport: **visa-free** for a set number of days (often 30, 60, or 90), **visa on arrival** (you pay and get a stamp at the airport), or **apply in advance** (an embassy visa or an online e-visa). Which bucket you're in depends entirely on your nationality, so check the destination's official immigration portal before booking flights.

## ETAs — pre-approval for visa-free visitors

Several countries ask even visa-free visitors to get an **Electronic Travel Authorization** before departure — Korea's K-ETA, the US ESTA, and the upcoming EU ETIAS are examples. It's a quick online form, not a visa. Apply early on the *official* government site and avoid look-alike paid agents that charge extra.

## Digital arrival cards

The paper landing card is disappearing. Many destinations now use an online **arrival card** you complete a few days before you land, with your passport, flight, and accommodation address. Being visa-free or ETA-exempt usually does not exempt you from it — check whether your destination requires one.
