## Know the local numbers

There's no single worldwide emergency number, so save your destination's before you land. **112** works across the EU and on most GSM phones (often even without a local SIM); **911** is North America; **999** the UK; and many countries split police, fire, and ambulance across separate numbers. Several also offer a multilingual **tourist hotline** that can interpret and relay to emergency services. The country guides below list the exact numbers.

## Medical care & insurance

For a serious emergency, call the local ambulance number. For minor issues, pharmacies handle common ailments almost everywhere, and big cities usually have clinics or hospitals with English-speaking staff. Crucially, **most countries make foreign visitors pay out of pocket** — buy travel insurance covering medical treatment and evacuation before you go, and keep the assistance number handy.

## Staying safe

Normal precautions travel well anywhere: watch your belongings in crowds, use licensed taxis or a trusted ride-hailing app, keep a **copy of your passport** separate from the original, and note your **embassy or consulate** contact in case you lose documents or need help. Check your government's travel advisory for the destination before you book.
