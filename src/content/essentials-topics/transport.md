## Transit cards

Almost every major destination has one rechargeable tap card that covers the subway, city buses, and sometimes taxis and convenience stores — T-money in Korea, Suica/ICOCA in Japan, Oyster in London, EZ-Link in Singapore, Octopus in Hong Kong. Buy it at a station or convenience store when you land, load it with cash, and you've unlocked the cheapest way around town.

## City subways & buses

Big cities usually have clean, frequent, English-signed metro systems and extensive bus networks. Fares are distance-based and low. The main gotcha is navigation: Google Maps transit is excellent in most countries but weak in a few (use Naver Map or Kakao Map in Korea), so check your destination's guide.

## Between cities

Where it exists, **high-speed rail** is usually the best way between major cities — comfortable, central-to-central, and often faster than flying once you count airport time (Korea's KTX, Japan's Shinkansen, Europe's TGV/Frecciarossa). Intercity buses reach smaller towns the train doesn't.

## Taxis & ride-hailing

A local ride-hailing app usually beats flagging a cab for price transparency and to avoid language barriers over your destination — Kakao T in Korea, Grab across Southeast Asia, DiDi in China, Uber/Bolt in much of Europe and the Americas. The country guides note which one works where you're going.

## Airport transfers

Most large airports have a dedicated rail link or official airport bus that's far cheaper than a taxi into town. Buy your transit card at the airport first, then take the train or bus. See the per-country guide for the exact option.
