## Read the climate first

Temperate destinations (much of Korea, Japan, Europe, the US) have four seasons, and the sweet spots are typically **spring and autumn** — mild, dry, and scenic. Tropical and monsoon destinations (Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia, much of India) instead have a **dry season** and a **rainy season**; the dry months are usually the comfortable time to go.

## Peak, shoulder, and off-season

Peak season has the best weather but the biggest crowds and highest prices. **Shoulder season** — just before or after the peak — is often the smart choice: good conditions, thinner crowds, better value. The off-season is cheapest, but check *why* it's quiet (monsoon, extreme heat or cold) before you book.

## Time it to a highlight

Some trips are worth planning around a specific moment — **cherry blossoms** (roughly late March–April across Korea and Japan), **autumn foliage** (late October–November), or a major **festival**. These dates shift a little each year with the weather, so check a current forecast close to your trip, and browse our [upcoming events](/events) for dated highlights.
