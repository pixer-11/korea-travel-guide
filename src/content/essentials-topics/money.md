## Local currency

Every destination has its own currency and price level — a coffee that's a few dollars in one country is a fraction of that in another. Check a live exchange rate before you go rather than trusting airport boards, and learn the rough value of the local notes so prices sanity-check at a glance. Each country guide names the currency and typical costs.

## Cards vs cash

Card and contactless acceptance varies enormously: some countries (Korea, Sweden, Singapore) are effectively cashless, while others still lean on cash for markets, taxis, temples, and small family-run places. The safe approach almost everywhere is a contactless card for shops and restaurants plus a modest cash buffer for the places that don't take plastic.

## ATMs & exchange

Withdraw local cash from bank ATMs or ones marked for foreign/"Global" cards, which usually offer an English menu. The big money-saver: when an ATM or card terminal offers to bill you in your *home* currency, decline and choose the **local** currency — the "convenience" conversion bakes in a poor rate. A card with no foreign-transaction fee helps most.

## Tipping & tax refunds

Tipping is cultural, not universal — generous in the US, light in much of Europe, and simply not done in Korea or Japan. Many countries also offer **tax-free shopping**: keep receipts above the threshold and claim the VAT/GST refund at the airport before departure. The per-country guide spells out both.
