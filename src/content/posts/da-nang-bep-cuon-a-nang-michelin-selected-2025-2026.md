## Why go

Bếp Cuốn Đà Nẵng has become one of the most talked-about restaurants in the city, and its inclusion in the Michelin Selected list for 2025-2026 has only intensified that reputation. With an unusually high 4.9-star rating across more than 17,000 reviews, it's not a hidden gem anymore — it's a benchmark for what modern, casual Vietnamese "cuốn" dining can look like. The concept centers on cuốn, the broad family of Vietnamese rolls and roll-your-own dishes built on rice paper, fresh herbs, rice vermicelli, and various grilled, steamed, or marinated proteins. Rather than a single dish, expect a build-your-own experience: platters of herbs, pickled vegetables, dipping sauces, and rice paper arrive alongside the main proteins, and diners assemble their own rolls at the table.

## Getting there

The restaurant sits at 31-33 Trần Bạch Đằng, in the An Hải area on the eastern side of the Han River, a district known for its stretch of riverside restaurants and proximity to the city's beach corridor. It's roughly a 10-15 minute taxi or Grab ride from the Dragon Bridge and the Han River pedestrian bridge area, and a similarly short ride from My Khe Beach, making it easy to combine with an evening beach walk or a river-view stroll before or after dinner. Given the restaurant's popularity, ride-hailing apps like Grab are the most reliable way to arrive without hassle over trying to flag a taxi streetside. There's no metro system in Da Nang, so taxi, Grab, or motorbike are the standard ways to get around, and Trần Bạch Đằng is easy for any driver to locate given the restaurant's visibility and foot traffic.

## What to eat

The menu leans into cuốn variations: expect items built around grilled pork, shrimp, and other proteins wrapped fresh at the table with rice paper, herbs like mint and perilla, and a range of dipping sauces from fish sauce-based nước chấm to peanut-based blends. Because the format is interactive, portions are typically shared across the table rather than ordered individually per person, so groups of 3-4 get the most out of the range of proteins and wraps on offer. Price level sits in the mid-range bracket for Da Nang dining — not a street-food price point, but well below upscale hotel restaurants — so budgeting around 200,000-400,000 VND per person for a full meal with drinks is a reasonable expectation, though exact menu prices should be confirmed on-site or via the restaurant's current listings, as these can shift.

## When to go

Given the volume of reviews and its Michelin Selected status, this is a restaurant that fills up, especially at peak dinner hours between 6:30 and 8:30 pm. Arriving right at opening in the early evening, or eating a slightly later lunch, is the more comfortable way to experience it without a long wait. Weekdays are noticeably calmer than weekends. Da Nang's dry season, roughly February through August, is the most pleasant stretch for evening dining generally, since outdoor or semi-open seating areas common in Vietnamese restaurants are more comfortable without the heavier rains of the autumn months (September through December tends to bring the city's wettest, stormiest weather).

## Nearby & tips

Pair a visit with a walk along the An Hải riverside promenade, a look at the Dragon Bridge (which breathes fire and water on weekend nights), or an evening at My Khe Beach nearby. Because the restaurant thrives on a build-it-yourself format, first-timers unfamiliar with assembling cuốn shouldn't hesitate to ask staff for a quick demonstration — it's a normal request and makes the meal far more enjoyable. Reservations, where possible through the restaurant's official channels or a hotel concierge, are worth arranging given the crowd levels; walk-ins should have a backup plan or be prepared to wait during peak hours.
