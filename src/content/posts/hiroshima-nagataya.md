## Why Nagataya

Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki is stacked, not mixed — a thin crepe, a mountain of shredded cabbage, pork belly, egg, and springy yakisoba or udon noodles, all pressed together on a teppan griddle and finished with a tangy-sweet sauce. Nagataya is one of the city's respected addresses for this dish, with a reputation built over decades and a Google rating of 4.4 from over 3,300 reviews — a strong signal in a city with hundreds of okonomiyaki shops competing for the same tourists and regulars. What sets it apart for international visitors is that it's one of the relatively few okonomiyaki spots in central Hiroshima to explicitly offer vegan and vegetarian versions, swapping the pork and traditional sauce components so plant-based travelers don't have to skip the city's signature dish.

## Getting there

Nagataya sits in Otemachi, 1-chome, in Naka Ward — the central district that also holds the Peace Memorial Park and Museum, just a few minutes' walk to the south. The address (1-chome-7-19, Shiraishi Building 1F) puts it within easy reach of Hondori and Chuden-mae stations on the Hiroshima Electric Railway (the city's iconic tram network, often just called "the streetcar"), both a short walk away. If arriving from Hiroshima Station, take any tram bound for Hiroshima Port or Eba and get off at Chuden-mae or Fukuromachi; the ride takes roughly 15-20 minutes. Walking from the Peace Memorial Park takes about 10 minutes, making it a natural lunch stop after visiting the museum and A-Bomb Dome.

## What to eat

Start with the standard "Hiroshima-yaki" (sometimes just listed as okonomiyaki) with pork and soba noodles — it's the benchmark dish and the one the restaurant is best known for. Many regulars add a squeeze of extra sauce and a shake of the dried seaweed or bonito flakes provided at the counter. If you're vegetarian or vegan, ask specifically for the plant-based version; Nagataya is noted for accommodating this, though portion and ingredient details can vary, so confirm with staff what's swapped out (usually the pork and sometimes the sauce base). Portions are generous enough that one okonomiyaki per person is usually plenty, though it's common to order a side of edamame or a cold beer to go with it. Seating is typically at a counter facing the teppan, so you can watch the cook build each layer — part of the appeal of eating okonomiyaki in Hiroshima rather than ordering it elsewhere.

## When to go

Lunchtime (roughly 11:30am–1:30pm) and early evening tend to draw the heaviest local crowds, especially on weekends, so arriving right at opening or in the mid-afternoon lull can mean a shorter wait. As with most small, popular Hiroshima okonomiyaki counters, seating is limited, and the restaurant doesn't always take reservations for walk-in-style counter dining — check current hours and whether reservations are accepted before making a special trip, since hours can shift seasonally or around holidays.

## Nearby & tips

Pair a visit with the Peace Memorial Park and Museum (10 minutes' walk), the A-Bomb Dome, or a wander through Hondori, Hiroshima's main covered shopping arcade, which is close enough to combine into one afternoon loop. Because Nagataya is a teppan-counter restaurant, expect a casual, low-frills atmosphere rather than table service — part of the charm, and typical of the genre. Price level is moderate (in the ¥¥ range), and like many smaller local restaurants in Japan, having cash on hand is still a sensible backup even if card payment is accepted. If you want to compare styles, Hiroshima's Okonomimura building (a multi-floor okonomiyaki food court) is a short tram ride away, but Nagataya's steady 4.4-star reputation makes it a solid single stop if you only have time for one.
