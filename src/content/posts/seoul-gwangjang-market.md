Gwangjang Market is one of the oldest traditional markets in Seoul, and for
visitors it's the single best place to eat your way through Korean street food
in one afternoon. This guide covers what to order, how much to bring, and the
practical details you actually need.

## What to eat

- **Bindaetteok (mung bean pancake)** — the market's signature dish, fried
  fresh at stalls near the main entrance.
- **Mayak gimbap ("addictive" mini seaweed rolls)** — tiny, cheap, and served
  with a mustard-soy dip.
- **Yukhoe (seasoned raw beef)** — for the more adventurous.

## When to go

Late morning to early afternoon on weekdays is the sweet spot — you'll beat
the heaviest crowds while stalls are freshly stocked. Most dishes run a
budget-friendly ₩3,000–₩10,000, so bring some cash for the stalls that don't
take cards.

## Getting there

Take Seoul Metro **Line 1 to Jongno 5-ga Station (Exit 8)** — the market is a
two-minute walk. A T-money transit card makes this trivially cheap; see our
[transport guide](/essentials/transport).
