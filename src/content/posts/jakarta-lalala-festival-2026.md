## Why go

LaLaLa Festival has become one of Jakarta's headline international music weekends, drawing an audience that mixes Southeast Asian festival-goers with fans flying in specifically for the lineup. The 2026 edition is set for August 22–23, 2026, at Jakarta International Expo (JIExpo), and the announced headliners include Steve Lacy, Two Door Cinema Club, Kodaline, The Flaming Lips, Rex Orange County, Honne, and Flo — a lineup that spans indie rock, alt-pop, psych-rock, and R&B-leaning acts, so it appeals to a broader crowd than a single-genre festival. If you're building a Southeast Asia trip around live music, this is one of the region's bigger tentpole events, roughly on par with other multi-day festivals in Manila, Singapore, or Kuala Lumpur in terms of international drawing power.

## Getting there

JIExpo is in Kemayoran, Central Jakarta, a large convention-and-exhibition complex that regularly hosts concerts, trade fairs, and the Jakarta Fair. It's roughly 20–30 minutes by car from central Jakarta (Sudirman/Thamrin area) depending on traffic, and about 45–60 minutes from Soekarno-Hatta International Airport (CGK) outside peak hours — but Jakarta traffic is notoriously unpredictable, so budget extra time, especially on festival nights when roads around Kemayoran get congested with rideshare drop-offs. Grab and Gojek are the easiest way to get around; for arrivals from the airport, the Damri airport bus or a pre-booked airport taxi are reliable alternatives to street-hailing. There's no MRT or LRT station directly at JIExpo, so most attendees rely on ride-hailing apps or festival shuttle services if organizers announce them closer to the date — check the official LaLaLa channels for shuttle points from central hubs like Grand Indonesia or Sudirman.

## What to expect on-site

Expect the standard large-format festival layout: multiple stages spread across JIExpo's expansive outdoor and covered halls, food and beverage vendors (a mix of Indonesian street-food-style stalls and international festival fare), merchandise booths, and designated smoking and rest areas. Jakarta in August sits in the dry season, so expect hot, humid days and warm nights — light, breathable clothing, a refillable water bottle, and sun protection for daytime sets are worth planning for, while a light rain layer is still sensible since tropical downpours can happen even outside peak wet season. Given the two-day format with a headliner-heavy bill, arriving early for a stage you care about is wise, as overlapping set times are typical at festivals this size.

## Tickets and practical planning

Ticket categories, exact pricing, and set times have not been finalized in the information available here, and festival ticket tiers (early bird, regular, VIP, day passes) typically change as the date approaches. Do not rely on secondhand resellers — LaLaLa's official website and verified social media accounts are the only sources to confirm pricing, entry gates, bag policies, and age restrictions. Because this is a large-scale international event, popular tiers and VIP packages have sold out in past editions, so early booking is generally advisable once sales open.

## Nearby & tips

Kemayoran itself is mostly commercial and exhibition space, but it's a short ride from central Jakarta's hotel districts (Menteng, Sudirman, Thamrin) and from Kota Tua (Jakarta's Old Town) if you want to sightsee before or after the festival days. Grand Indonesia and Plaza Indonesia malls are good options for a pre-festival meal or last-minute supply run (earplugs, ponchos, portable phone chargers). If flying in specifically for LaLaLa, consider booking accommodation in the Sudirman–Thamrin corridor for easy ride-hailing access rather than staying too close to JIExpo itself, since options directly around the venue are limited. As with any large outdoor event in a tropical city, check the weather forecast a few days out and confirm the final schedule, gate times, and entry requirements on LaLaLa's official site before you finalize travel plans.
