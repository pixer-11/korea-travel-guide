## Why go

HONNE — the London duo behind atmospheric bedroom-soul hits like "Warm on a Cold Night," "Someone That Loves You," and "Location Unknown" — is marking a full decade together with a multi-night run in Manila, a rare instance of an international act playing three consecutive shows in the Philippines rather than a single stop. The Pasay dates are billed as a celebration of their catalog, mixing career-spanning favorites with reworked or reimagined versions of older songs, which suggests a setlist built for longtime fans rather than a standard promo-cycle show. For Southeast Asian fans, multi-night runs like this are uncommon enough that they tend to draw attendees flying in from across the region, not just Metro Manila.

## Getting there

The show is set for PICC Plenary Hall, part of the Philippine International Convention Center complex in the Cultural Center of the Philippines (CCP) Complex, Pasay City — the same reclaimed-land arts district that houses the CCP main building, the Metropolitan Museum, and Star City. It sits just off Roxas Boulevard, close to Manila Bay.

There's no direct MRT/LRT line to the CCP Complex, so most visitors arrive by car, taxi, or ride-hailing app (Grab is the standard in Manila). If coming from the airport (NAIA), it's roughly a 15–20 minute drive barring traffic — budget more during rush hour or event load-out times. From Malate or Ermita, a taxi or Grab along Roxas Boulevard is straightforward. Those coming from the Makati or Ortigas business districts should treat this as a 30–45 minute trip depending on traffic, which in Manila can be unpredictable regardless of time of day.

## What to expect

Details on set times, opening acts, and merchandise are typically released closer to the date, so don't lock in a itinerary assuming a fixed start time yet. What is confirmed: three consecutive nights (August 10, 11, and 12, 2026), with the promise of reworked arrangements alongside the hits — meaning even fans attending multiple nights should expect some variation rather than an identical repeated show. Given PICC Plenary Hall's function as a seated/standing convention-style venue rather than a dedicated concert arena, expect a more intimate sound experience than an arena show, with capacity considerably smaller than say, the Mall of Asia Arena a few minutes away.

## When to go and how to plan

August falls in Manila's wet season, so pack a light rain jacket or umbrella regardless of which night you attend, and build in extra transit time for possible flooding-related traffic delays. If you're choosing among the three nights, note that opening night (August 10) and closing night (August 12) often carry different atmospheres — first-night energy versus a farewell-show feel — though HONNE hasn't publicized which night carries any special elements.

## Nearby & practical tips

The CCP Complex area gives you easy access to Manila Bay's sunset views along Roxas Boulevard, a five-minute Grab ride to the SM Mall of Asia complex (dining, the Mall of Asia Arena, and the bay-side promenade), and proximity to Pasay's hotel row for those not wanting to cross the city post-show. Since this is a multi-night run, consider booking accommodation in Pasay or nearby Parañaque rather than commuting from Quezon City or Makati each night — traffic after a show lets out can be substantial.
