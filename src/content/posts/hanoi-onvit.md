## Why ONVIT Matters

ONVIT is one of the more unusual dining stories to come out of Hanoi in recent years: a Korean fine-dining restaurant that made history as the first Korean restaurant in Vietnam to be awarded a Michelin Star, recognized in the 2026 Michelin Guide cycle. That distinction places it in a small, closely watched category of restaurants in the city — Hanoi's Michelin presence is still young, having only been introduced a few years ago, so any new star draws attention from both the local dining scene and international food travelers plotting a trip around it.

What makes ONVIT distinct isn't just the Korean pedigree — it's the fusion angle. The kitchen builds a tasting menu that draws on Korean technique and presentation while incorporating Vietnamese ingredients and flavor notes, a hybrid approach that reflects Hanoi's growing cross-cultural dining identity as more Korean expats, chefs, and investment have moved into the city over the past decade.

## Where to Find It

ONVIT operates inside the Grand Plaza Hanoi Hotel, located on Tran Duy Hung street in the Cau Giay district — a business and residential area southwest of the Old Quarter that has become one of Hanoi's newer commercial hubs, home to office towers, embassies, and upscale hotels. This puts ONVIT outside the typical tourist trail of the Old Quarter and French Quarter, so visitors should plan a dedicated trip rather than expecting to stumble onto it while wandering.

Because it's housed within a hotel, the restaurant benefits from hotel-standard service infrastructure — valet or hotel parking, a formal dining room, and staff accustomed to international guests — which is worth knowing if you're deciding what to wear or how to arrive.

## Getting There and Booking

Cau Giay district is a reasonable taxi or ride-hailing (Grab) trip from the Old Quarter — expect roughly 20 to 30 minutes depending on traffic, which in Hanoi can be unpredictable during rush hour (7:30–9:00 AM and 5:00–7:00 PM). There is no major metro line serving this stretch of Tran Duy Hung directly as of now, so taxi, Grab, or hotel car is the most practical way to arrive.

Because ONVIT is newly Michelin-starred, reservations should be treated as essential, not optional — newly recognized starred restaurants routinely see booking demand spike immediately after an award is announced. Contact the restaurant or the Grand Plaza Hanoi Hotel concierge directly to confirm current opening hours, seating availability, and any dress code well before your visit, since these details can shift as the restaurant adjusts to its new profile.

## Nearby & Practical Tips

Cau Giay isn't a traditional sightseeing district, but it's within reach of Hanoi's Botanical Garden and the West Lake (Ho Tay) area, both a short ride away, making it feasible to pair a dinner at ONVIT with an afternoon walk around West Lake's cafes and temples. If you're staying in the Old Quarter, budget extra travel time both directions and consider booking a return taxi in advance, since flagging one down late at night in this part of the city can take longer than in the tourist core.
