## Why go

The Collective Pattaya sits inside the Holiday Inn Pattaya, right on Beach Road, and has built a reputation as one of the city's most consistent buffet-and-à la carte restaurants. A 4.8 average from over 2,173 reviews is an unusually strong showing for a hotel restaurant that serves both guests and walk-ins — most large-format buffets struggle to keep quality even, but this one leans on live cooking stations (think noodle bars, grilled seafood, made-to-order eggs) rather than just steam trays, which is usually what separates a good buffet from a forgettable one. It's a practical choice if you're staying anywhere along Beach Road or Central Pattaya and want a single stop that covers Thai, Japanese, Western, and international comfort food for a table with mixed tastes — kids, seafood lovers, and picky eaters included.

## Getting there

The address is 463/99 Beach Road (Beach Rd), Muang Pattaya, Bang Lamung, Chon Buri 20150 — inside the Holiday Inn Pattaya, on the beach side of the road. If you're coming from South Pattaya or Central Pattaya, it's a short walk or a few minutes by baht bus (the shared songthaew pickup trucks that run up and down Beach Road for a flat local fare). From Walking Street, expect roughly a 10–15 minute walk north along the beachfront promenade. Taxis and Grab drivers know the Holiday Inn well since it's a beachfront landmark; just tell the driver "Holiday Inn Pattaya, Beach Road" rather than the restaurant name to avoid confusion. If you're arriving from Suvarnabhumi or U-Tapao airport, it's roughly a 90-minute or 30–40 minute drive respectively, so factor that into meal timing if you're day-tripping in.

## What to eat

The draw here is the buffet format — international spreads that typically rotate a seafood-on-ice station, sushi and sashimi, a carving station, Thai curries and stir-fries, and a dessert table with local fruit alongside Western bakes. Breakfast buffets at hotel restaurants like this tend to be the best value relative to effort: you get everything from congee and dim sum to eggs cooked to order and pastries, which suits jet-lagged travelers on different schedules. Dinner is usually the bigger production, often built around themed nights (seafood or barbecue-style spreads are common at hotel buffets in this category), so if you're set on a specific theme, it's worth confirming that night's lineup with the hotel before you go. Beyond the buffet, an à la carte menu is typically available for those who'd rather order individual Thai or international dishes rather than commit to the full spread.

## When to go

Breakfast (roughly 6:30–10:30 AM in most Pattaya hotel restaurants) is quieter and a good option if you want space at the stations without queuing. Dinner service picks up considerably after 7 PM as both hotel guests and outside diners arrive, so arriving right at opening or booking a table in advance is the more comfortable route on weekends and during Thai and Chinese public holidays, when Pattaya's hotel occupancy spikes. Since exact hours, buffet themes, and pricing shift seasonally, confirm the current schedule and cost directly with the Holiday Inn Pattaya before heading over, especially if you're timing a visit around a specific themed night.
