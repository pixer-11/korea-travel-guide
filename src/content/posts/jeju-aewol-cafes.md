## What to know

Aewol is a coastal town on the northern side of Jeju Island, known for a stretch of shoreline lined with independent cafes, many built to take advantage of sweeping views of the ocean and, on clear days, distant cliffs and horizon. The "cafe street" isn't one single road so much as a scattered cluster of cafes along the coastal route and side lanes near Aewol Harbor, ranging from small minimalist spaces to larger multi-level buildings with rooftop or window seating facing the sea.

The area has become popular precisely because it offers a slower, more scenic alternative to Jeju City's commercial districts — think architecture-forward interiors, big glass windows, and outdoor terraces designed for lingering over coffee while watching the waves. It's a trendy spot, so expect a mix of locals, domestic tourists, and international visitors, especially on weekends.

## How to get there

Aewol is roughly 20–30 minutes by car from Jeju City, making it an easy half-day or full-day trip. Public buses run along the coastal road connecting Jeju City to Aewol and beyond, though schedules can be infrequent, so a rental car or taxi offers more flexibility, especially if you plan to hop between multiple cafes or continue on to other coastal towns afterward. Renting a car is the most common approach for visitors covering Jeju's coastline in one trip, since it lets you stop freely along the way for photos or unplanned detours.

## Tips

- **Visit on a weekday or early in the day** if possible — parking near the most scenic stretches can be limited and fills up quickly on weekends and holidays.
- **Walk or cycle along the coast** rather than driving between every cafe; many of the best views and photo spots are along the walking paths between cafes, not just inside them.
- **Dress for wind** — Jeju's coastline, including Aewol, is often breezy, and window or terrace seating can feel cooler than expected even on sunny days.
- **Go for the view, stay for the pace** — this is less about ticking off a specific cafe and more about enjoying an unhurried coastal atmosphere, so build in extra time rather than rushing between stops.
- **Combine with a coastal walk** — the area works well paired with a stroll along Aewol's shoreline paths or a broader day trip along Jeju's northern coast.
- **Carry cash and cards** — while many places accept cards, having some cash on hand is a reliable general travel habit in more rural or coastal parts of Jeju.

## What to expect

Because the cafe scene here rotates and evolves with trends, exact offerings change frequently. Rather than seeking out one specific name, plan to explore a few blocks on foot, compare the views and interiors from the outside, and choose based on what atmosphere and seating appeals to you that day.
