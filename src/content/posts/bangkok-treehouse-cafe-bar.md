## Why go

Bangkok has no shortage of concept cafés, but Treehouse Cafe & Bar has earned a rare 4.9-star average across more than 2,600 reviews — a signal that the hype holds up. The draw is the setting: a multi-level space built around lush greenery, hanging vines, wooden walkways, and warm fairy-light canopies that make it feel like a treehouse retreat dropped into the middle of the city. It photographs beautifully, but it's also a legitimate spot to eat and drink well, not just a backdrop for a five-minute photo stop.

## Getting there

The address is 20 Sukhumvit 31, in Khwaeng Khlong Tan Nuea, Watthana district — one of Bangkok's most walkable, expat-friendly pockets, sandwiched between the Sukhumvit and Ploenchit corridors. The easiest approach is via **Phrom Phong BTS station** (Sukhumvit Line), from which it's roughly a 10–15 minute walk north into the Soi 31 network of smaller lanes. If you'd rather skip the walk in Bangkok's heat, a metered taxi or Grab from the station takes just a few minutes. Asok BTS/MRT interchange is also within reach if you're coming from the Sukhumvit MRT line side, adding a bit more walking time. Given the soi (side-street) address, expect to duck off the main road into a quieter, tree-lined residential lane — this is typical of Bangkok's best-hidden cafés and part of the charm.

## What to see and eat

Inside, the multi-tiered layout means every visit can feel different depending on where you're seated — ground-floor tables sit closer to the greenery walls, while upper platforms lean into the "treehouse" framing with wooden beams and rope details overhead. Expect a menu that spans café standards (specialty coffee, fruit-forward drinks, desserts) through to more substantial Thai and Western dinner plates and a cocktail list once the sun goes down, which is why the "Cafe & Bar" name fits — it functions as a laid-back daytime coffee stop and shifts into a bar-lounge atmosphere by night. Portions and presentation tend to be styled for the aesthetic as much as the palate, so come as much for the ambience as for a specific dish. Because exact menu items and prices shift seasonally, treat any online menu as a rough guide and confirm current offerings and prices on arrival.

## When to go

Weekday afternoons (roughly 2–5pm) are the best window to enjoy the greenery without a wait, since the space's Instagram-friendly design draws steady foot traffic, especially from local visitors and photographers on weekends and evenings. If you want the fairy-light, golden-hour version of the space, aim for the late-afternoon-into-early-evening transition — you'll catch natural light for photos, then watch the lighting shift as evening service and the bar menu kick in. Given the sky-high review count, arriving without a reservation on a Friday or Saturday night is a gamble; call ahead or check for a booking system if visiting on a weekend.

## Nearby & tips

Sukhumvit Soi 31 and the surrounding Soi 33 and Soi 39 lanes are dotted with quiet residential streets, boutique restaurants, and other café hideaways, making this a good area to wander before or after your visit rather than rushing in and out. Phrom Phong itself sits near EmQuartier and Emporium malls, useful if you want air-conditioned shopping or a food court fallback nearby. Wear breathable clothing — much of the seating is semi-outdoor and Bangkok's humidity is constant regardless of season — and bring a portable charger if you're planning an extended photo session, since natural light fades fast under the canopy after dusk. Budget at least an hour, longer if you're staying for a full meal or cocktails.
