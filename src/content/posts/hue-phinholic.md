## Why PhinHolic Matters in Hue's Café Scene

Hue is better known for imperial tombs and citadel walls than for coffee culture, but a new generation of cafés is changing that — and PhinHolic, on Bến Nghé street, is one of the names locals and young Vietnamese travelers now mention first. The name is a pun on "phin," the small metal drip filter that is the backbone of Vietnamese coffee, and it signals the concept: traditional slow-drip brewing dressed up in a design-forward space. With a 4.5 rating across nearly 900 reviews, it has become a reliable stop for travelers who want a break from temple-hopping without sacrificing craft or atmosphere.

## Getting There

PhinHolic sits at 65 Bến Nghé, in Thuận Hóa ward, on the south side of the Perfume River — the newer, more commercial half of Hue where most mid-range hotels cluster. It's roughly a 10–15 minute walk from Dong Ba Market and the Trang Tien Bridge, and about the same distance from the Le Loi hotel corridor. If you're staying near the Citadel on the north bank, cross via Trang Tien or Phu Xuan Bridge and continue south for 10–15 minutes on foot, or take a short Grab (Vietnam's ride-hailing app) or metered taxi ride, which should run only a few minutes and a small fare given how compact central Hue is. Cycling is also common here — many hotels rent bicycles, and Bến Nghé's street layout is flat and easy to navigate.

## What to Order and What to Expect

The signature draw is phin-brewed coffee served the traditional way, with the metal filter still perched on the glass so you watch it drip. Order the classic ca phe sua da (iced coffee with condensed milk) for the baseline experience, or try one of the shop's trendier riffs — salted cream coffee (an update on the "cà phê muối" style that originated in Hue itself) or coconut-coffee blends are typical of this genre of café and worth asking about by name. Price level is moderate for Vietnam — expect most drinks in the 25,000–55,000 VND range — and payment by cash or bank QR transfer is standard. The space itself leans into clean, photogenic interiors: exposed materials, greenery, and seating arranged for both solo laptop use and small groups, which explains its popularity with younger domestic tourists and the social-media crowd. Plan for 45 minutes to an hour if you're just having a drink, longer if you want to work or linger.

## When to Go

Hue's climate is hot and humid for much of the year, with a pronounced rainy season roughly October through December, so late morning (10–11am) or later afternoon (around 4–6pm) tends to be the most comfortable window, avoiding both midday heat and downpour risk. Weekday mornings are noticeably quieter than weekend afternoons, when local students and groups fill the seating. If you want photos of the space without crowds, aim for opening hours; check current hours locally since small Hue cafés sometimes adjust seasonally.

## Nearby & Practical Tips

Combine a stop here with the Dong Ba Market (10–15 minutes away) for a browse through produce, dry goods, and street food stalls, or walk the riverside promenade along the Perfume River at sunset before or after your coffee. Because Thuận Hóa is the commercial south bank, you're also within easy reach of Hue's night market strip and river-cruise docks if you want to build a half-day loop: citadel in the morning, PhinHolic for a midday break, market and river walk in the evening. As with most independent Hue cafés, exact hours and menu prices can shift, so confirm specifics on-site or via a map app before making it the centerpiece of your schedule.
