## Why go

ChinaJoy is one of Asia's largest digital entertainment expos, covering console and PC gaming, mobile games, esports, animation, cosplay, VR/AR, and increasingly AI-driven entertainment tech. It draws major domestic publishers (Tencent, NetEase, miHoYo have historically had a presence) alongside international hardware brands, peripheral makers, and streaming platforms. For industry visitors it's a dense few days of business meetings and product reveals; for the public segment it's a spectacle of elaborate booths, playable demos, merchandise, and one of the world's most photographed cosplay scenes. If your interest is either the business side of China's gaming industry or you simply want to see the scale of Chinese fan culture up close, this is the single best annual showcase for it.

## Getting there

ChinaJoy has for years been held at the National Exhibition and Convention Center (NECC) in Shanghai's Hongqiao area, one of the largest exhibition venues in the world — but venue placement can shift year to year, so treat this as a strong likelihood rather than a certainty for the 2026 edition. The NECC sits near Hongqiao Transportation Hub, which combines Hongqiao Railway Station (high-speed rail) and Hongqiao International Airport (domestic and some international flights), making it easy to reach directly from other Chinese cities or connect onward. Metro Line 2 and Line 10 both serve Hongqiao Railway Station/Hongqiao Airport Terminal 2 stations, with the exhibition center a further shuttle or short taxi ride away during major events — event organizers typically run shuttle buses from the transport hub on show days. If you're flying internationally into Shanghai, Pudong International Airport is the other option, roughly 1.5–2 hours from Hongqiao by metro or maglev-plus-transfer, so factor that into arrival planning.

## What to expect on-site

Historically ChinaJoy splits into a B2B (trade) area for industry professionals and a B2C (public) area for general ticket holders, often with separate ticketing, separate hours, and sometimes separate days or halls. Expect long queues at popular publisher booths in the mornings, extensive cosplay photography around entrances and side halls (arrive early if you want uncluttered shots), and a strong secondary market for exclusive merchandise and figures that can sell out within hours of doors opening. Booth demo lines for high-profile new game titles are typically the single biggest time sink — if there's a specific game or panel you want, plan to queue well before the hall opens rather than after your first stop.

## When to go

The dates for 2026 are set for July 31 through August 3 — a Friday through Monday span, suggesting the show likely front-loads trade/business activity early in the week and public days toward the weekend, following the format of past years. Shanghai in late July/early August is hot and humid (routinely in the low-to-mid 30s°C with high humidity), so the mix of outdoor queuing and un-air-conditioned crowd crush inside halls can be physically demanding — pace yourself and expect the exhibition center's food and drink prices to run higher than average local rates.

## Nearby & tips

Hongqiao itself isn't a tourist district, but it's well connected by metro to central Shanghai attractions like the former French Concession, Jing'an Temple, and the Bund, all reachable within 30–45 minutes, making it feasible to combine ChinaJoy days with sightseeing days rather than staying purely event-focused. Hotels near Hongqiao Hub fill up fast and prices spike during the expo window, so book accommodation as early as possible once dates are confirmed. Because venue, hall assignments, ticket categories, and exact opening hours can change from the historical pattern, always check the official ChinaJoy website (and any official app or WeChat account) shortly before travel to confirm venue, ticket purchase channels, and day-by-day schedule.
