## Why the Cable Car Tops Yeosu's List

Yeosu built its reputation on being a port city wrapped around water, and the Yeosu Maritime Cable Car is the single best way to see that geography in one sweep. The line runs roughly 1.5 km across Yeosu Bay, connecting Jasan Park on the mainland side to Dolsan Park on Dolsan Island. From the cabin you get an uninterrupted view of the harbor, the cable-stayed Dolsan Bridge lit up at dusk, and — on clear days — Odongdo Island jutting into the sea like a green comma. Two cabin types are offered: standard enclosed cabins and glass-bottom cabins, the latter requiring a bit more nerve and usually a longer wait.

## Getting There

Yeosu doesn't have a subway system, so getting to the cable car means bus or taxi from Yeosu Expo Station (KTX terminus), which is the transit hub most visitors arrive at. From the station, a taxi to the Jasan Park boarding station takes about 15–20 minutes; city buses also run this route but check current numbers at the station's tourist information desk, since routes are periodically renumbered. If you're coming from central Yeosu (near Yeosu Harbor or the Expo Ocean Park), the ride is shorter, closer to 10 minutes by taxi.

Many visitors combine the cable car with a walk across Dolsan Bridge itself or a stop at Hyangiram Hermitage further out on Dolsan Island, a cliffside Buddhist hermitage famous for sunrise views — though that's a separate trip requiring its own bus or taxi ride of 30–40 minutes.

## What to See and Do Once You're There

- **Jasan Park side**: Landscaped walking paths and a small observation deck before you even board; this is also where most food stalls and cafés cluster.
- **The ride itself**: About 13 minutes one-way at a leisurely pace, giving time to photograph the harbor, the bridge, and the fishing boats below.
- **Dolsan Park side**: A hillside park with its own lookout point over the bay — worth 20–30 minutes of wandering before heading back.
- **Odongdo Island**: A short drive or bus ride from central Yeosu, this small island connected by a breakwater is known for camellia trees (blooming roughly December–March) and a lighthouse at its tip. It's a natural add-on to a cable car day since both sit near the harbor.
- **Yeosu Night Sea Bus / Harbor cruises**: Boat tours departing near the Expo area let you see the same bridge and cable car from the water, especially striking after dark when Yeosu's "night view" (an unofficial but locally promoted specialty) lights up the coastline.

## When to Go

Late afternoon into early evening is the sweet spot: you ride while there's still daylight for harbor views, then linger on the Dolsan side as the bridge lighting comes on. Spring (April–May) and autumn (October–November) bring the mildest weather and clearest skies for photography. Summer is busy with domestic tourists and can mean long queues, particularly for the glass-bottom cabins; winter is quieter but colder on the exposed platforms, so dress warmly if riding after sunset.

## Nearby & Practical Tips

Budget half a day just for the cable car and Dolsan/Jasan parks, or a full day if you're adding Odongdo and a harbor dinner of Yeosu's signature dish, *gejang* (soy-marinated raw crab) or fresh sashimi from the harbor market. Cabins run on a queue system that can back up on weekends and holidays — arriving right at opening or in the hour before sunset tends to mean shorter waits. As with any seasonal attraction, confirm current operating hours, cabin prices, and whether the glass-bottom line requires separate booking before you go, since these details shift year to year.
