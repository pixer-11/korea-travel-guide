## Why go

Labuan Bajo's harbor is lined with seafood restaurants catering to travelers heading to or from Komodo National Park, and Taman Laut Handayani is one of the town's best-known names for this style of dining. The draw is straightforward: display cases of fresh fish, prawns, squid, crab, and lobster that you select yourself, priced and weighed on the spot, then grilled or wok-fried to order with Indonesian seasonings like sambal, sweet soy (kecap manis), and butter garlic. With a 4.6 rating across more than 5,700 reviews, it's firmly established as one of the highest-volume seafood addresses in Labuan Bajo, not a quiet find — expect a lively, full dining room most evenings.

## Getting there

The restaurant sits at Jl. Ande Bole No. 9, in central Labuan Bajo within Komodo District, Manggarai Barat regency, Nusa Tenggara Timur. Labuan Bajo's compact harbor town means most hotels and guesthouses are within a 10–20 minute walk or a short ojek (motorbike taxi) ride away. If you're staying near the main strip along Jl. Soekarno Hatta or around the waterfront, a taxi or app-based ride isn't strictly necessary — many visitors simply walk down toward the harbor in the late afternoon. If arriving from Komodo Airport (Labuan Bajo Airport), it's roughly a 10-minute drive into town; arrange a car or ojek through your hotel or a ride-hailing app.

## What to see and eat

The format here is pick-your-own: walk the ice display, choose your fish or shellfish by weight, and specify the cooking style — bakar (grilled), goreng tepung (battered and fried), or saus padang/asam manis (spicy or sweet-sour sauce) are common choices at Labuan Bajo seafood houses. Standouts to look for include red snapper, grouper, squid, and prawns, often served alongside kangkung (water spinach) plecing, rice, and sambal on the side. Because pricing is by weight and market rate, seafood costs can vary night to night — ask staff to confirm the per-kilogram price before they cook it, since this is standard practice at Indonesian seafood restaurants and avoids surprises on the bill. The restaurant's setting near the water also makes it a natural spot to time a meal around sunset, one of Labuan Bajo's genuine daily spectacles over the harbor and outer islands.

## When to go

Evenings are the primary service window, as is typical for seafood restaurants in this region, with the restaurant filling up as tour boats return from day trips to Komodo, Padar, or Pink Beach in the late afternoon. Aim to arrive by 6 to 6:30 pm if you want a table with a sunset view before the post-tour crowd peaks around 7 to 8 pm. Given the volume of reviews and its popularity with both domestic and international travelers, weekends and peak dry-season months (roughly April through October, when boat tours to Komodo are busiest) tend to be the most crowded.

## How to visit like a local

Locals and repeat visitors alike treat pick-your-own seafood spots like this as a negotiation and confirmation process, not a fixed menu: state your seafood choice, ask the price per kilo, and confirm the total weight before it's cooked — this is normal practice, not haggling out of place. Payment is generally by cash (Indonesian rupiah) or card, though smaller seafood joints in Labuan Bajo can be cash-preferred, so carrying rupiah as backup is a sensible habit rather than a hard rule. Tipping isn't obligatory in Indonesia, though rounding up or leaving small change is appreciated. The most common mistake tourists make at busy seafood restaurants like this one is arriving right at peak dinner hour without a plan — walk-ins can face a wait as tables turn over slowly for groups sharing multiple seafood dishes. Since this is a high-traffic, well-reviewed restaurant rather than an undiscovered spot, arriving slightly earlier or slightly later than the 7–8 pm rush, or requesting a table when you enter rather than waiting to be shown one during the busiest window, are the more effective strategies for a smooth visit. Ordering family-style with a few dishes to share, as most tables around you will be doing, is the norm rather than single-portion ordering.

## Nearby to pair with your visit

After dinner, the nearby waterfront promenade and Kampung Ujung area make for an easy post-meal stroll, and many of Labuan Bajo's dive shops and tour operators booking Komodo National Park trips are clustered within a few minutes' walk, making it simple to book or confirm a boat trip for the following day while you're in the area.
