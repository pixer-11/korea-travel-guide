## What Sabato's is

Sabato's is the buzzy Italian-American restaurant tucked inside Kimpton Naluria, the Kimpton hotel that anchors the Tun Razak Exchange (TRX) financial district in Kuala Lumpur. The concept leans hard into a 1970s New York feel — think disco-era energy, retro signage, and a red-sauce menu built for sharing rather than fussy fine dining. It's the kind of place designed to be loud, nostalgic, and unapologetically social, which sets it apart from the more restrained, minimalist dining rooms that dominate KL's upscale scene.

Since Kimpton hotels are known for boutique, design-forward properties with a playful streak, Sabato's fits that DNA: it's less "hotel restaurant" and more a standalone destination that happens to live inside one. Expect a soundtrack and interior that do a lot of the storytelling — vintage-inspired lighting, disco-ball nods, and a dining room built to feel like a downtown NYC supper club rather than a hushed KL hotel lobby restaurant.

## Getting there

TRX is one of Kuala Lumpur's newest business and lifestyle districts, purpose-built with its own MRT interchange — TRX MRT station on the Kajang Line, which also connects to the Circle Line (MRT2), making it one of the more straightforward new-development destinations to reach without a car. From the station, Kimpton Naluria and the TRX Exchange TRX mall complex are a short, well-signed walk through the development's elevated walkways, which is useful during KL's frequent afternoon downpours. If arriving by car or e-hailing (Grab is the standard app locally), the hotel has porte-cochère drop-off, and TRX has multiple paid parking structures tied to the mall.

Because Sabato's sits within a hotel in a still-developing district, it's worth confirming the exact entrance and whether the restaurant is accessed from the hotel lobby or a separate street-level entry — signage in newer KL developments can lag behind opening buzz.

## What to expect at the table

The menu draws on Italian-American red-sauce classics — the genre that made Sabato's-style restaurants a staple of old-school New York dining: think generous portions, family-style pastas, and comfort-forward dishes rather than delicate small plates. The restaurant's identity is built around abundance and nostalgia rather than austere fine dining, so come hungry and ready to share rather than expecting a tasting-menu pace.

Given its status as a newly opened, trending spot, exact menu items and prices aren't fixed points to rely on here — treat the specific dishes and price range as something to check via the restaurant's own channels or the Kimpton Naluria website closer to your visit, since new openings often adjust their menus in the first months.

## When to go and how to plan

As a new, high-energy destination restaurant inside a boutique hotel, Sabato's is likely to draw both hotel guests and KL's dining-out crowd, especially on weekend evenings. If you want the room at its most atmospheric — full, loud, disco lighting doing its job — a Friday or Saturday dinner slot is the move. For a marginally calmer experience while still catching the vibe, a weeknight dinner is worth trying.

Reservations are strongly advisable for a trending opening like this one; call or book through the hotel or Sabato's own listing rather than assuming walk-in availability, particularly in its first year when curiosity-driven demand tends to be highest. Always double-check current opening hours and reservation policy directly with Kimpton Naluria before setting out, since newly launched restaurants often adjust their schedules.

## Nearby & tips

TRX itself is worth building an evening around: the district includes TRX Park, an elevated green space, and TRX City's retail mall, so a pre- or post-dinner walk is easy to fold in. The area is also a quick MRT ride from KLCC and Bukit Bintang if you want to bookend dinner with KL's more established nightlife or shopping. Dress code is likely smart-casual given the retro-lounge concept, but confirm with the hotel if you're planning a special-occasion visit.
