## What is Dongpirang?

Dongpirang is a small hillside village in Tongyeong known for its ever-changing collection of murals painted on the walls, stairways, and exterior surfaces of homes that climb the slope above the old town. The name is thought to relate to the neighborhood's position on the eastern side of a rocky outcrop overlooking the harbor. What began as a grassroots art project to revitalize an aging residential area has grown into one of Tongyeong's best-known creative spaces, with local and visiting artists periodically repainting sections, so the murals reflect a mix of styles and themes rather than a single fixed gallery.

Because it's a lived-in neighborhood, expect narrow lanes, steep steps, laundry hanging from balconies, and everyday residential life alongside the art. This authenticity is part of the appeal, but it also means visitors should be mindful of noise and privacy as they walk through.

## How to get there

Dongpirang sits close to Tongyeong's central harbor area, within walking distance of Jungang (Central) Market and the old downtown core. Most visitors combine a Dongpirang walk with a stop at the market and the nearby harbor, since they're all clustered in the same part of the city. If arriving by intercity bus, you'll typically need a short taxi ride or local bus connection into the old town center, then proceed on foot up the hillside lanes. Local tourist maps and signage in the area typically point out the mural trail entrances near the market.

## What to expect

- The village is arranged on a slope, so the walk involves a fair number of stairs and inclines.
- Murals vary in size and subject, from whimsical characters to scenic and abstract designs, and are repainted or refreshed periodically, so the experience differs by season and year.
- Several vantage points along the paths offer views over Tongyeong's harbor and the surrounding rooftops, a nice reward for the climb.
- Small cafes and shops are scattered through the area, useful for a rest stop.

## Tips

- **Footwear matters**: flat, supportive shoes make the stairs and slopes far more comfortable.
- **Timing**: early morning or late afternoon light is kinder for photos and cooler for walking; midday sun on the exposed hillside can be intense in summer.
- **Pace yourself**: there's no single "correct" route—wandering the alleys and doubling back is part of the experience.
- **Respect residents**: keep voices down near homes and avoid photographing private doorways or windows directly.
- **Pair it with the market**: Jungang Market's proximity makes it easy to combine a mural walk with people-watching and local food browsing.
- **Bring cash**: small vendors and cafes in older neighborhoods may not all accept cards.

## Why it's worth a visit

Dongpirang offers a low-cost, flexible way to see a creative, community-driven side of Tongyeong beyond its harbor and seafood reputation—ideal for travelers who enjoy street art, photography, and slow neighborhood wandering.
