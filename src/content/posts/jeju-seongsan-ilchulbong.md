## Why Go

Seongsan Ilchulbong — "Sunrise Peak" — is a tuff cone that erupted from the seabed roughly 5,000 years ago, its walls now forming a dramatic bowl-shaped crater rimmed with jagged rock spires above the sea. It's one of Jeju's three UNESCO World Natural Heritage sites (alongside the Geomunoreum Lava Tube System and Hallasan National Park) and the island's most photographed silhouette — the volcanic cone that appears on postcards and posters of Jeju. Unlike Hallasan, this is a short, high-reward climb: most visitors are up and down within 1 to 1.5 hours, making it an easy add-on rather than a full-day commitment. The name refers to its reputation as one of Korea's best sunrise-viewing spots, though the crater itself, the sweeping coastal views, and the surrounding green pastureland make it worth a visit at any hour.

## Getting There

Seongsan Ilchulbong sits on Jeju's easternmost tip, in Seongsan-eup, Seogwipo, at some distance from both Jeju City and central Seogwipo, so a rental car is the most practical option — there's a large paved parking lot at the base. Without a car, intercity buses run from Jeju City's bus terminal and from Jeju International Airport toward Seongsan; travel time is roughly 1 to 1.5 hours depending on traffic and route, and the peak is a short walk from the Seongsan bus stop into the entrance area. Many visitors combine a rental car day-trip covering the whole eastern coast, since this corner of the island clusters several sights within a 15-20 minute drive of each other.

## What to See and Do

The climb follows a paved and staircase path up the seaward side of the cone — steep in sections but short, typically 20-30 minutes up and a similar time down. There is no route into the crater bowl itself; the trail leads to an observation platform on the rim, where you look down into the grassy, saucer-shaped depression and out across the ocean toward Udo Island's low profile on the horizon. The rock formations lining the summit — sometimes likened to a crown — are a highlight for photos, especially with the sea and Jeju's coastline as backdrop.

At the base, the surrounding fields and the adjacent seaside village are part of the experience: this is also where Jeju's haenyeo (female free-divers) traditionally worked, and haenyeo performance shows are sometimes staged near the entrance, worth checking for a schedule on arrival. The nearby coastline and Gwangchigi Beach, within walking distance, offer a flatter, easier contrast to the climb, with photogenic yellow canola flowers blanketing the fields in spring.

## When to Go

Sunrise is the signature experience — the peak's name is no accident — and arriving before dawn rewards early risers with a quieter climb and a view of the sun rising directly over the crater rim and ocean. That said, sunrise crowds can be substantial, especially around New Year's Day, so arrive with time to spare for parking and the walk up. Outside of sunrise hours, late afternoon and early evening tend to be less crowded than midday, and the golden-hour light on the cliffs is worth the timing. Spring (canola blossoms) and autumn (silver grass, cooler hiking weather) are generally considered the most comfortable seasons; midsummer brings heat and humidity on the exposed, largely shade-free trail, and windy days are common given the coastal exposure, so a light windbreaker is worth carrying regardless of season.

## Nearby & Practical Tips

Entry requires a paid ticket at the base — prices are modest but subject to change, so confirm current admission and opening/closing times (which shift seasonally) before heading out, as the gate typically closes well before actual sunset or sunrise to allow time for the climb. Budget 1 to 1.5 hours total for the visit including photos at the summit. Nearby attractions worth pairing with Ilchulbong include Udo Island (reachable by ferry from Seongsan Port, a short drive from the peak), Seopjikoji coastal walking area, and Jeju Folk Village. Wear real walking shoes — the paved trail has uneven, sometimes slippery sections, particularly after rain.
