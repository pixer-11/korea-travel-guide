## Why Ci Siamo Matters Right Now

Ci Siamo isn't a legacy institution — it's a newer addition to Danny Meyer's Union Square Hospitality Group portfolio, the same team behind Union Square Cafe, Gramercy Tavern, and Shake Shack. What's put it on every traveler's radar in 2026 is a single ranking: it landed at No. 1 on Yelp's Top 100 Places to Eat in the U.S., a list that draws on millions of reviews nationwide. For a New York restaurant to top a national list, rather than just a local "best Italian in NYC" roundup, is unusual, and it's why Ci Siamo has shifted from "well-regarded neighborhood spot" to "reservation you plan a trip around."

The kitchen's calling card is Rigatoni Alla Gricia — the Roman classic built on guanciale, Pecorino Romano, and black pepper, done without tomato so the technique has nowhere to hide. It's the dish reviewers and food writers mention first, and it's a reasonable barometer for whether the rest of the menu is worth exploring on a return visit.

## Where It Is and How to Get There

Ci Siamo sits in the Hudson Yards/Manhattan West corridor on Manhattan's far west side, a part of the city that didn't really exist as a dining destination a decade ago and has since filled in with towers, retail, and USHG's brand of hospitality. The area is walkable from the High Line's northern end and from Hudson Yards' shops and public plaza, making it easy to combine with an afternoon of sightseeing before or after a meal.

By subway, the closest station is 34th St–Hudson Yards on the 7 line, which drops you within a short walk of the Manhattan West complex. The A/C/E at 34th St–Penn Station and the 1/2/3 lines are also within reasonable walking distance if you're coming from other parts of Midtown or the West Side. Given the neighborhood's density of new office towers, weekday lunch traffic on foot and by subway can be heavy, so build in extra time if you have a firm reservation window.

That reputation, paired with the Yelp ranking, means walk-in availability is unlikely, especially at dinner.
