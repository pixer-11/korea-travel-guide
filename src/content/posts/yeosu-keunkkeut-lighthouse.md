## Why go

큰끝등대, or Keunkkeut Lighthouse, sits at the edge of Pyeongsa-ri on Dolsan Island, one of the peninsulas that make Yeosu's coastline so jagged and photogenic. Unlike the city's headline attractions — Odongdo Island, the Yi Sun-sin Bridge, or Hyangiram Hermitage — this lighthouse rarely appears on standard itineraries, which is exactly its appeal. It's a small, functional navigation light rather than a monument, but its position at the "big end" (큰끝, the literal meaning) of the headland gives it an unobstructed sweep of open water toward the Namhae Strait. With a 4.3 rating across 124 reviews, it's clearly known to a loyal circle of local and domestic travelers, even if it stays off most English-language maps.

## Getting there

The address places it at San 1-1, Pyeongsa-ri, Dolsan-eup — on the rural southeastern coast of Dolsan Island, which connects to downtown Yeosu via the Dolsan Bridge. There's no direct subway or train access (Yeosu itself is reached by KTX to Yeosu Expo Station or by intercity bus), so from central Yeosu the most practical option is a taxi or a rental car, roughly a 30–40 minute drive depending on traffic across the bridge and down the island's coastal roads. Public buses do run through Dolsan-eup, but connections to Pyeongsa-ri are infrequent, so check current schedules before relying on one. Drivers should expect the final stretch to be a narrower rural road; park near the village and walk the remaining path down to the point, since the lighthouse itself sits past the main road on the headland's edge.

## What to see and do

The lighthouse structure is modest — a compact white tower typical of Korea's minor coastal navigation lights — so the real draw is the setting. The rocky point offers a clear, wide-angle view of the sea, with fishing boats and, on clear days, distant islands visible on the horizon. It's a good spot for photographers chasing long-exposure seascapes or simply for travelers wanting a quiet place to sit by the water without crowds. There are no ticket booths or facilities at the site itself, so this is a bring-your-own-water-and-snacks kind of stop. Because it's an active navigational lighthouse, treat it as a place to view from the surrounding rocks and paths rather than a building to enter.

## When to go

Sunset is the most rewarding time, since the lighthouse faces open water and the coastline picks up warm light in the evening; sunrise works too if you're staying overnight on Dolsan Island and want to beat the crowds elsewhere in Yeosu. Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) bring the mildest coastal weather for lingering at the point; summer is workable but expect humidity and stronger sun with little shade nearby. Winter visits are quieter still, though wind off the strait can be sharp, so dress for it. Whatever the season, checking a local sunset/sunrise time before heading out will help you time the walk down to the point.

## Nearby & tips

Dolsan Island rewards a half-day loop: pair 큰끝등대 with Hyangiram Hermitage, a cliffside Buddhist temple on the island's southern tip known for its own sunrise views, and the Dolsan Park overlook near the bridge, which has one of the best vantage points over Yeosu's night skyline. If you're driving the island's coastal roads anyway, budget extra time — the routes wind along the shoreline and are slower than they look on a map. Because the lighthouse itself has limited signage in English, having an offline map pin or downloaded directions to the exact coordinates in Pyeongsa-ri is worth doing before you set out. Combine the visit with fresh seafood in one of Dolsan's small coastal villages, many of which serve the day's catch at simple, unpretentious restaurants overlooking the water — a fitting way to close out a stop built around quiet, working coastline rather than manufactured sights.
