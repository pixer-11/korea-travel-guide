## Getting to Goyang Stadium

Goyang Stadium (also known as Goyang Sports Complex) sits in Ilsan, Gyeonggi Province, just northwest of central Seoul — close enough for a day trip but far enough that transit planning matters on concert nights. The area is served by Jeongbalsan Station and Madeul Station on Seoul Subway Line 3 (Orange Line), both within walking distance to a short taxi ride of the stadium complex; check which exit is closest once the venue confirms concourse access points for this specific event. From central Seoul (e.g., Gangnam or Hongdae), figure on 45–60 minutes by subway. Expect the operator to add shuttle buses or extend late-night subway service for a three-day event of this size — watch official tour updates and Goyang city transit notices closer to the date, since large concerts here have prompted temporary shuttle routes in the past.

If you're flying in internationally, Incheon International Airport is roughly 40–60 minutes from Ilsan by airport limousine bus or car, often a more direct option than routing through central Seoul first.

## What to do around Ilsan

Goyang's Ilsan district is worth building a day around even if you're only in town for the concert. Ilsan Lake Park (Ilsandong-gu), one of the largest man-made lake parks in Asia, is a pleasant pre-show wander with walking trails and seasonal flower displays. The Hallyu World / Aqua Planet Ilsan aquarium and nearby Western Dom shopping and dining complex give fans a way to fill an afternoon before gates open. For food, Ilsan's Juyeop and Baekseok neighborhoods have a dense concentration of casual Korean restaurants, cafés, and convenience stores well-suited to a pre-concert meal without the crowds of central Seoul.
