## Why XLIII Is Worth the Detour

Da Nang has no shortage of coffee shops, but XLIII Specialty Coffee has built a reputation — nearly 9,500 Google reviews and a 4.7 rating — on being more than a caffeine stop. It's part of the city's growing wave of "destination cafés," where architecture, landscaping, and drink quality are all treated as equally important. Expect multi-level seating, greenery-heavy design, and enough distinct nooks (rooftop-style perches, garden tables, glass-walled rooms) that regulars often have a favorite corner. This is the kind of place locals bring out-of-town friends to show off Da Nang's contemporary café culture, rather than a quick roadside stop.

## Getting There

XLIII sits at Lô 419, 422 Ngô Thì Sĩ, in the Ngũ Hành Sơn district — the same area that's home to the Marble Mountains (Ngũ Hành Sơn), making it easy to combine both in one outing. From the main tourist strip along My Khe Beach or the Han River bridges, it's roughly a 10–15 minute ride by taxi, Grab, or motorbike, depending on your starting point. There's no metro system in Da Nang, so Grab (Vietnam's dominant ride-hailing app) is the most reliable way to get an exact fare and drop-off pin without language barriers. If you're staying near An Thuong or the beachfront hotels, a rented motorbike is a common option too — parking is generally available on-site or curbside.

## What to See and Order

The building itself is the main attraction: expect tiered outdoor terraces, tropical planting, and Instagram-friendly architectural details that make it a common stop for photos even among people who aren't big coffee drinkers. Inside, look for Vietnamese specialty drinks alongside international café staples — a well-made cà phê sữa đá (iced milk coffee), cold brew, or a fruit-based specialty drink are safe bets, and the "specialty" branding suggests single-origin or house-roasted beans are part of the draw. Given the price level (moderate-to-higher for the local market), this isn't the cheapest cà phê in town, but it reflects the space, service, and presentation you're paying for. Portions and seating are generous enough that ordering one drink and settling in for an hour or more is completely normal here.

## When to Go

Weekday mornings (roughly 8–10am) tend to be calmer, before both local regulars and tour groups arrive. Da Nang's climate means late morning through early afternoon can get hot and humid, especially from May through August, so shaded or air-conditioned indoor sections are worth requesting if you're visiting midday. Late afternoon into golden hour is popular for photography, given the tiered, plant-filled architecture — but expect more foot traffic and possibly a wait for the best-lit tables. If your trip includes the Marble Mountains, pair an early visit there (before the heat and crowds build) with a mid-morning coffee stop at XLIII afterward.

## Nearby & Practical Tips

Because it's positioned in Ngũ Hành Sơn rather than directly on the beach, XLIII works well as a midpoint stop between My Khe Beach and the Marble Mountains, both major fixtures of a Da Nang itinerary. Non-Vietnamese speakers shouldn't worry — café staff in Da Nang's trendy spots are generally accustomed to international visitors, and menus commonly include English. Bring a light layer if you're sensitive to strong air-conditioning indoors, and consider going with a small group if you want to try multiple drinks, since portion sizes and the range of specialty options make sharing worthwhile. As with any high-traffic café in Vietnam, confirm current opening hours before heading out, since seasonal adjustments and holiday closures aren't always reflected online.
