## What makes it stand out

The smash-burger technique itself is the headline: patties are pressed thin against a flat-top so the Maillard reaction does maximum work, creating a crust with more surface area (and more flavor) than a standard grilled patty. Pairing that with a potato bun — softer, slightly sweet, sturdy enough to hold juices without disintegrating — is a deliberate upgrade over the standard fast-food bun. Given the Scarpetta connection, expect the same attention to sourcing and plating that made its predecessor a word-of-mouth hit rather than just an Instagram trend.
