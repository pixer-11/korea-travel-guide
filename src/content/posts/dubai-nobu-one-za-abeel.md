## Why go

Nobu needs little introduction — the Nobu Matsuhisa-founded chain built its global reputation on Japanese cooking filtered through Peruvian technique, and this is its third location in Dubai. What sets the One Za'abeel branch apart isn't the menu so much as the setting: it occupies part of The Link, the record-breaking horizontal sky-bridge structure that joins One Za'abeel's twin towers about 230 metres above street level. That makes it one of the highest-set restaurants in a city that doesn't lack for altitude, with floor-to-ceiling views over the Downtown Dubai and DIFC skylines. For visitors who want the Nobu experience with a Dubai-specific reason to choose this branch over the others, the view is the differentiator.

## Getting there

One Za'abeel sits in the Za'abeel district, close to the Downtown/DIFC corridor and within easy reach of the World Trade Centre area. The nearest Dubai Metro stop is World Trade Centre Station on the Red Line; from there it's a short taxi or rideshare hop, since The Link sits well back from the street and isn't a direct walk-up. Taxis and ride-hailing apps are the simplest option for most visitors, especially at night when the tower is lit up and worth seeing from the road. Given the venue's height and security/lobby procedures typical of towers like this, build in extra time — arriving 15–20 minutes before your reservation is wise so you're not rushing through lobby check-in and elevators.

## What to eat

Expect the signature Nobu playbook rather than a radically different menu: black cod with miso is the dish the brand is built on, a sweet-savoury, butter-soft fish that's become a template copied citywide, and yellowtail jalapeño — thin-sliced yellowtail with jalapeño and a citrusy soy dressing — is the other essential order for anyone new to the restaurant. Beyond these two anchors, expect the broader Japanese-Peruvian format Nobu is known for: cold and hot appetizers meant for sharing, tiraditos and ceviche-style dishes, robata-grilled items, and a sushi/sashimi selection. Because this is a newly opened branch, exact menu pricing and any Dubai-specific dishes hadn't been widely documented at launch — treat the core dishes above as the reliable starting point and ask staff about branch-specific specials on arrival.

## When to go

Sunset seating is the obvious draw given the height and glass walls — arriving in the last hour of daylight lets you watch the transition from daytime haze to the illuminated night skyline without moving tables. Dubai's cooler months, roughly November through March, are the most comfortable time to visit the city generally, and this period will likely coincide with continued high demand following the January 2026 opening, so weekend evenings should be booked especially far ahead. Weeknight dinners, if available, tend to be a slightly easier reservation than Thursday–Saturday evenings, which function as the city's de facto weekend.

## Nearby & tips

One Za'abeel's other amenities — including additional dining and The Link's public viewing elements — make it easy to pair a Nobu dinner with a broader visit to the tower rather than a single-purpose trip. The Downtown Dubai cluster, including Dubai Mall and the Burj Khalifa area, is a short taxi ride away, so many visitors combine an afternoon there with an evening reservation here. Because this location opened only recently and is described as one of the city's most in-demand tables, reservations should be made as far in advance as the booking system allows, and it's worth confirming current opening hours, dress code, and reservation policy directly with the restaurant before finalizing plans — details that shift in a venue's first months of operation.
