As a food court, Third Street's strength is variety concentrated in one spot: expect a mix of stalls covering South Asian, Filipino, Arabic, and fast-food-style options, the kind of lineup that reflects MBZ City's diverse resident population. The smart approach is to skip the idea of a single "main dish" and instead order two or three smaller plates from different counters — a grilled or fried main, a rice or biryani-style dish, and something to share, like a plate of fresh juice or a dessert to close. Portions at food courts like this tend to be generous and prices modest compared to central Abu Dhabi dining, though exact menu prices vary by stall and are worth confirming on the spot rather than assuming.

## How to visit like a local

Pay attention to how orders work here: at most stalls within a food court like this, you order and pay directly at each counter rather than through a central till, so carry small cash denominations (dirhams) even if some stalls accept cards — cash keeps things moving and avoids awkward waits at quieter counters. Tipping isn't expected at counter-service stalls, though rounding up or leaving small change is appreciated if service was attentive. There's rarely a formal queueing system beyond standing at the counter you want, so watch how regulars order — usually a quick, direct request rather than an extended conversation. The most common visitor mistake is treating it like a sit-down restaurant with a single menu: instead, walk the full length of the court first, see what's fresh and busy (a good proxy for quality at food courts), and then decide.
