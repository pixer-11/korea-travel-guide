## What to know

Anmok Coffee Street sits along Anmok Beach in Gangneung, a coastal city on South Korea's east coast in Gangwon Province. Gangneung has built a reputation as the unofficial coffee capital of Korea, and this stretch of beachfront cafés is the clearest expression of that identity. Rather than a single attraction, it's a cluster of café buildings — many with large glass windows, rooftop terraces, and multiple floors — arranged along the sand, so the "sight" is really the combination of ocean view, coffee culture, and casual beach-town energy.

The street works well any time of year. In summer it gets lively with beachgoers and evening crowds; in winter the cafés offer a warm, glass-walled perch to watch waves roll in without the summer crowds. Weekday mornings and early afternoons tend to be quieter, while weekends and sunset hours draw the biggest crowds, especially at cafés with upper-floor or rooftop seating.

## How to get there

Gangneung is reachable from Seoul via the KTX high-speed rail (Gangneung line) in a little over two hours, or by intercity bus. From Gangneung Station or the bus terminal, Anmok Beach and its coffee street are a short taxi or local bus ride away — the area is on the coast, west of the city center's main hub, so it's not walkable from the station. Local buses connect the city center to Anmok Beach, and taxis are inexpensive for the short hop if you're short on time. Many visitors combine a stop here with other Gangneung coastal attractions along the same shoreline, since the beach road makes it easy to walk between different points.

## Tips

- **Go for the view, not just the coffee.** Seating on upper floors or terraces facing the water is the main draw — if a café looks crowded downstairs, ask about seating upstairs.
- **Walk before you sit.** The beach itself is part of the experience; a walk along the sand between café visits is a nice way to break up the afternoon.
- **Expect variety, not uniformity.** Cafés here range from minimalist and modern to more elaborate multi-floor buildings, so it's worth strolling the length of the street before committing to one.
- **Bring cash or a card that works easily in Korea** — most cafés accept card payment, but having some cash as backup is a good habit anywhere outside major chains.
- **Parking fills up on weekends and holidays**, so those without a car may find the bus or taxi option less stressful than driving.
- **Pair it with a sunset visit.** The east-facing coast means sunrise views are also notable, but sunset café-hopping with the beach lit up is a popular reason people linger into the evening.

Overall, treat Anmok Coffee Street as a relaxed half-day add-on to a Gangneung itinerary — come hungry for a slow coffee break with an ocean backdrop rather than a packed sightseeing checklist.
