## Why go

Oasis 21 is Nagoya's most photographed piece of modern architecture: an oval glass-and-steel canopy nicknamed the "Water Spaceship," floating above a sunken plaza and a working bus terminal in Higashi Ward. The glass roof holds a shallow layer of water that catches the sky and, after dark, reflects LED lighting in shifting colors. It's free to walk onto the elevated deck, and the structure frames a clean sightline to Nagoya TV Tower and Hisaya-odori Park just across the street, making it one of the easiest "wow" photo stops in the city without paying an admission fee.

## Getting there

Oasis 21 sits directly above Sakae Station, served by the Nagoya Municipal Subway Higashiyama Line and Meijo Line — take Exit 4 or 3 and you surface right inside the complex's underground level. It's also connected to Sakae Station on the Meitetsu Seto Line. Because the ground floor doubles as a bus terminal for local and highway buses, Oasis 21 is a natural transit hub if you're arriving from elsewhere in Aichi Prefecture. On foot, it's about a 5–10 minute walk from the Nagoya TV Tower/Hisaya-odori Park area, and roughly 10 minutes from the Nagoya City Science Museum and Aichi Arts Center, both worth combining into a Sakae afternoon.

## What to see and do

The complex works on three levels. The underground "Galaxy Platform" holds shops, cafés, and direct access to the subway and bus terminal — useful for a quick meal or a rainy-day detour. At ground level is a green, sunken plaza used for markets, food events, and seasonal festivals. The signature experience is riding the escalator or glass elevator up to the elliptical rooftop deck, the "Water Spaceship," where you walk on glass panels beneath (and partly through) the shallow reflecting pool. There's no admission charge to access the deck, though some special exhibits or events inside the complex occasionally charge a small fee — worth confirming locally if a specific event is on. Around the perimeter, expect a mix of casual dining, cafés, and clothing shops rather than a full department-store experience; it's more of a transit-plaza-meets-observation-deck than a shopping mall.

## When to go

Sunset through the first hour of darkness is the peak window: the glass roof turns amber, then the LED lighting under the water layer activates, and the TV Tower behind it lights up too, giving the best contrast for photos. Weeknight evenings are calmer than weekend evenings, when local couples and groups treat the deck as a casual date spot. During cherry blossom season and winter illumination periods (roughly November through February in many years), the surrounding Hisaya-odori Park often runs light displays that pair well with a visit here — check seasonal event calendars before you go, since exact dates shift annually. Daytime visits are fine for architecture photos and people-watching but lack the signature glow.

## Nearby & tips

Sakae is Nagoya's central nightlife and shopping district, so Oasis 21 works well as an anchor point: Nagoya TV Tower and Hisaya-odori Park's Central Park zone are a short walk north, Sakae's underground shopping arcades (Sakae Chika) connect from the same station complex, and Nagoya Castle is a further subway ride away on the Meijo Line if you want to extend the day. Because the deck is outdoors and the "water" pool is shallow rather than swimmable, it can get slippery after rain — take the glass elevator rather than the escalator if you're carrying luggage or traveling with small children. Restrooms, coin lockers, and seating are available on the underground level, making it a practical rest stop even if you're just passing through Sakae Station between subway lines.
