## Why go

Steki is a rarity in Fujairah's restaurant scene: a dedicated Greek taverna doing classic Mediterranean cooking — moussaka, souvlaki, saganaki, grilled octopus, fresh salads with feta and olives — steps from the water on Al Faseel Road. With a 4.8 rating from close to 600 reviews, it's clearly winning over diners without being a mobbed tourist stop, which makes it one of the more satisfying finds in a city better known for its corniche and mountain scenery than its dining. The price level sits in the moderate range, so it's realistic for a proper multi-course Greek meal without fine-dining prices.

## Getting there

Steki sits on Al Faseel Road (E99), close to Umbrella Beach in Fujairah's Al Faseel area, a short drive from the city's main corniche stretch. Fujairah doesn't have a metro system, so the practical options are taxi, ride-hailing app, or a rental car — all easy since the E99 is a well-known coastal road running along the emirate. If you're coming from Fujairah city center or the airport, expect roughly a 10-15 minute drive depending on traffic; from Khor Fakkan or Dibba to the north, budget more like 30-45 minutes along the coastal highway. There's generally enough space to park nearby given the beachfront setting, but arrive a little early on weekends when the beach itself draws crowds.

## What to eat

Lean into the Greek classics here rather than ordering off-menu Middle Eastern dishes you can find elsewhere in Fujairah. Start with a shared meze spread — think tzatziki, dips, and warm pita — then move to saganaki (fried cheese) or grilled octopus if seafood is on offer that day. Moussaka and souvlaki (chicken or pork substitutes, given the region, are common on Gulf-based Greek menus) are the dishes reviewers consistently single out. Finish with baklava if it's available. Portions at Greek tavernas tend to be generous and built for sharing, so order fewer mains than the table size suggests and add extra small plates instead.

## Best time to visit

Fujairah's climate makes outdoor and beachfront dining most pleasant from October through April, when evening temperatures drop enough to enjoy a meal without the summer humidity. Within the day, an early dinner — arriving as the sun starts to set over the mountains, roughly between 6 and 7pm — gives you the best light for the beach views and beats the later dinner rush. Lunch is a quieter alternative if you'd rather avoid any wait altogether, since Gulf dining culture skews toward late evening meals starting after 8pm.

## What's nearby

Umbrella Beach itself is the obvious pairing — a walk along the shore before or after your meal costs nothing and lets you settle a heavy Greek meal. Fujairah's corniche, with its walking paths and views of the Gulf of Oman, is a short drive away, as is Fujairah Fort and the Al Bidyah Mosque (one of the UAE's oldest mosques) if you're combining dinner with a half-day of sightseeing. Fujairah Museum is another nearby stop for context on the emirate's history before or after eating.

## How to visit like a local

Because Steki is still under the radar rather than a fully discovered destination, you won't need the aggressive queue strategies common at Dubai's hyped restaurants — but a few habits will still smooth the visit. Call ahead or book if the restaurant offers reservations, since seating directly tied to a beach location is often limited and fills up on weekend evenings (Thursday and Friday nights, the Gulf's weekend, are the busiest). Cash and card are both generally accepted in the UAE, and a service charge may already be added to the bill, so check before tipping extra — a modest additional tip in cash is appreciated but not obligatory. Dress is casual but the setting is a proper sit-down meal, not fast-casual, so beachwear alone isn't appropriate. The most common visitor mistake is treating it as a quick beach snack stop and rushing the order — Greek tavernas are built for a slow, shared, multi-plate meal, so come with time and an appetite rather than ordering a single main and leaving.
