## Why go

LaLaLa Fest has built a reputation across Asia as a festival that mixes global indie and alt-pop names with strong production values, and the 2026 Manila stop brings that formula to the Philippines with Steve Lacy and Two Door Cinema Club topping the bill. For international fans, this is a rare chance to catch acts of this caliber without needing a Coachella-sized travel budget — Manila is a well-connected regional hub, and the show is contained to a single-day event on August 21, 2026, making it feasible as a long weekend trip built around one big night out.

## Getting there

The venue is the World Trade Center Manila, a long-running convention and events complex on Roxas Boulevard in Pasay City — the same district that hosts the Mall of Asia, the SMX Convention Center, and Manila's diplomatic and embassy row. Pasay sits between Manila's historic core and the airport, which is a practical advantage: Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA) is roughly a 15-20 minute drive away depending on terminal and traffic, making this one of the more airport-adjacent major venues in the city.

For public transit, the closest rail options are LRT-1's EDSA or Libertad stations and MRT-3's Taft Avenue station, all of which require a short tricycle, jeepney, or ride-hailing (Grab) hop to reach WTC itself, since the complex isn't directly on a rail line. Given Manila's traffic patterns, especially on a festival evening, budget extra time if arriving by car or taxi during rush hour, and consider Grab or taxi directly from your hotel rather than mixing rail and road transport if you're unfamiliar with the area.

## What to expect on-site

WTC Manila is a large indoor/hybrid events complex, historically used for trade expos, concerts, and large-scale conventions, so expect a big-room festival experience — proper stage, sound, and lighting infrastructure — rather than an outdoor field setup. Steve Lacy, known for genre-bending guitar-driven R&B and hits like "Bad Habit," and Two Door Cinema Club, the Northern Irish indie rock trio behind "What You Know" and "Something Good Can Work," represent two very different live sets, so plan your evening around both if you want the full range of the bill — check final set times once the official day schedule is released.

Beyond the two headliners, LaLaLa Fest events in other Asian cities have typically featured a broader supporting lineup added closer to the date, so it's worth watching official announcements for additional acts before finalizing which sets are must-see versus skippable.

## When to go and how to plan around it

August in Manila falls squarely in the southwest monsoon (habagat) season, meaning heavy rain and even typhoon risk are real possibilities — pack a compact rain layer, waterproof phone pouch, and build slack into your travel dates in case of weather-related schedule shifts, which do happen with outdoor-adjacent events in the Philippines during this period. Since WTC has substantial indoor space, the show itself is less likely to be rained out than an open-field festival, but getting to and from the venue in monsoon downpours is its own logistical hurdle.

## Nearby & practical tips

Pasay's SM Mall of Asia complex is a five-to-ten-minute drive from WTC and is a convenient base for pre-show dinner, ATMs, and last-minute essentials, with a wide spread of Filipino and international dining options. Hotels along Roxas Boulevard and in the nearby Bay Area/Entertainment City strip (Okada, Solaire, City of Dreams) put you within a short ride of the venue and give you a fallback for after-party options once the show wraps.
