## Why go

Tales by Chapter is one of the more unusual dining stories to emerge from Ho Chi Minh City's restaurant scene in recent years: a plant-based, rooftop-to-table concept that has been awarded a Michelin Green Star for 2026. The Green Star is Michelin's distinction for restaurants combining excellent food with genuinely sustainable practice — not just recycled napkins, but a real closed-loop model. Here that means produce grown on-site on the restaurant's own rooftop garden, supplemented by ingredients sourced from a dedicated eco-farm in Đà Lạt, Vietnam's cool-climate highland region famous for its produce, coffee, and flowers. For visitors who want to taste where Vietnamese fine dining is heading beyond pho and bánh mì stalls, this is a name worth knowing.

## What makes it stand out

The "chapters" in the name hint at a tasting-menu format that unfolds course by course, though exact dishes and pricing aren't published widely enough to quote with confidence — expect that to shift seasonally given the farm-to-table sourcing model. What is consistent, and central to the Green Star recognition, is the zero-waste philosophy: minimizing food miles by growing on the rooftop, minimizing waste through composting and full-ingredient use, and building relationships with a single eco-farm supplier rather than sourcing anonymously. This is plant-based cooking positioned as a culinary destination in its own right, not a side note on a broader menu — worth knowing if you're building an itinerary around Vietnam's evolving sustainable-dining scene alongside more traditional street-food crawls.

## Getting there

The restaurant sits on Nguyễn Thành Ý, a small street in Đa Kao ward, District 1 — the same central district that holds Notre-Dame Cathedral, the Reunification Palace (Independence Palace), and the Jade Emperor Pagoda, all within a 10–15 minute walk or short taxi ride. This puts Tales by Chapter comfortably within the compact core most visitors already base themselves in. Grab-hailing (Vietnam's dominant ride-hailing app) is the easiest way in for most travelers, since exact street numbers on small District 1 lanes can be tricky for traditional taxis to find on the first pass. If you're touring on foot from the Reunification Palace or Turtle Lake area, it's a flat, walkable stretch through a residential-meets-café pocket of the district.

## When to go and what to expect

Given its rooftop component, an early evening visit timed for sunset — before Ho Chi Minh City's heat fully breaks but as the light softens — tends to be the most rewarding for atmosphere. As with any restaurant riding a fresh Michelin distinction, demand for tables has likely surged since the 2026 Green Star announcement, so reserving ahead rather than walking in is the safer bet, especially for dinner service. Hours, seating policy, and menu structure should be confirmed directly through the restaurant's official channels or a hotel concierge shortly before your visit, since these details can change quickly for a newly recognized spot.

## Nearby & tips

Pair a visit with a slow afternoon around Đa Kao — the neighborhood has a scattering of independent cafés and small galleries — before or after your meal, rather than treating it as an isolated stop. Because the concept leans heavily on seasonal, farm-driven ingredients, don't expect a fixed, repeatable menu across visits; that variability is part of the point. If you're specifically chasing Vietnam's sustainable and Green Star-awarded dining scene, it's worth checking whether other Michelin Green Star or Bib Gourmand addresses in the city cluster near District 1 or District 3, so you can plan a walkable food-focused day rather than crisscrossing the city by taxi. Finally, dress is likely smart-casual given the fine-dining format, and given the rooftop element, a light layer for evening breezes isn't a bad idea even in Ho Chi Minh City's warm climate.
