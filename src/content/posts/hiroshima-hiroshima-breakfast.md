## Why go

Hiroshima's café scene tends to skew toward lunch and coffee-and-cake spots that open mid-morning at the earliest, which makes a dedicated breakfast destination unusual. Hiroshima Breakfast, tucked into a ground-floor unit in Dohashicho, has built a loyal following largely through word of mouth and a striking 4.9 rating across 175 reviews — the kind of consensus that's rare for any food business, let alone one serving the first meal of the day. For visitors staying in the downtown hotel cluster near Hiroshima's main shopping streets, it's a genuinely useful alternative to a hotel buffet: a chance to eat well, locally, and early, before the Peace Memorial Park and Atomic Bomb Dome fill with tour groups.

## Getting there

The address is 6-23-101, Dohashicho, Naka Ward, Hiroshima, on the first floor of its building — Dohashicho is one of the compact grid of streets just north of the Hondori covered arcade, Hiroshima's central pedestrian shopping street. If you're coming from Hiroshima Station, the easiest approach is by tram (streetcar): take one of the lines running toward Hiroshima-eki-mae and get off around the Kamiyacho-nishi or Kamiyacho-higashi stops, both a few minutes' walk from Dohashicho. Alternatively, it's a flat, walkable 20–25 minutes from the station if you'd rather see the city street-level. Because it sits within the downtown core, it's also an easy add-on if you're based near Peace Memorial Park, Shukkeien Garden, or Hiroshima Castle — all within roughly a 15–20 minute walk.

## What to expect

As the name promises, this is a breakfast-focused spot rather than an all-day café, which is worth noting because kitchens like this often have a narrower serving window than tourists expect. Expect a small, intimate room — the kind of scale that produces both the attentive service behind the review score and the likelihood of a short wait if you arrive after 9am. Japanese breakfast cafés of this type typically build their menus around simple, well-executed staples: thick-cut toast (shokupan) with butter or seasonal jam, soft-boiled or hard-boiled egg sets, a green salad, and hand-drip or siphon coffee, sometimes alongside a rotating specialty like French toast or a fruit sando. Menus and prices can change seasonally, so treat any specific dish name you hear in advance as a guide rather than a guarantee — confirm what's currently on offer once you're seated. If communication is a concern, having your hotel jot down the address in Japanese characters is a reliable backup for taxi drivers or in case you get turned around in the smaller side streets.

## When to go

Aim for an early slot, ideally opening time through mid-morning on a weekday. Because the space is small and the reputation strong, weekend mornings can mean a queue forms before doors open — arriving 10–15 minutes early is a reasonable hedge. Spring (cherry blossom season around late March–early April) and autumn foliage weeks bring heavier tourist traffic citywide, so a breakfast reservation-by-arrival strategy matters more during those windows. Hiroshima's summers are hot and humid by mid-morning, which is one more reason an early sit-down breakfast has appeal before heading out to walk the Peace Memorial Park.

## Nearby & tips

Pair the visit with a loop through Hondori arcade, a covered shopping street good for a post-breakfast coffee-to-go or browsing before museums open. Peace Memorial Park and the Atomic Bomb Dome are roughly a 15-minute walk southwest, making an early breakfast here a practical first stop before a morning at the park's museum. Shukkeien Garden and Hiroshima Castle sit to the north and are worth combining into a half-day loop. As with any small, high-demand café, hours can shift seasonally or with staffing, so it's worth confirming the day's opening time before setting out, especially on public holidays.
