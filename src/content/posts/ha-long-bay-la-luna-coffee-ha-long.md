## Why go

La Luna Coffee Hạ Long has built its reputation — a 4.4 rating across more than 2,000 reviews — on being one of the more photogenic, design-forward cafés in Bãi Cháy, the resort side of Ha Long Bay across the water from Hon Gai. It's the kind of place locals and visitors alike use for a coffee break framed by big windows, tiered terraces, and bay-adjacent views, rather than for the coffee alone. If you're spending a day in Bãi Cháy between boat tours, cable car rides, or beach time, it's an easy, comfortable stop to break up the sightseeing with something more relaxed and Instagram-friendly.

## Getting there

The café sits at 780 Hạ Long, right on the main road running through Bãi Cháy — the strip that hugs the shoreline and connects most of the area's hotels, the Sun World Ha Long cable car station, and Bãi Cháy Beach. Because Ha Long Bay doesn't have a metro or rail system, the practical ways in are on foot (if you're staying at one of the beachfront hotels along Hạ Long street), by taxi or Grab (Vietnam's main ride-hailing app, widely used here and generally cheap), or by rented motorbike. If you're coming from the old town side (Hon Gai) or from a cruise pier, expect a 15–25 minute taxi ride depending on traffic and exactly where you're dropped off. Given the address is a well-known stretch of road, any driver or Grab app will locate it without trouble.

## What to see and do

The draw here is the building itself and the setting: expect multiple levels or terraces designed to maximize views toward the water, plenty of glass, and seating arranged for both groups and solo visitors who want a quiet corner with a laptop or a book. Typical of Vietnam's trendy specialty-style cafés, the menu usually spans Vietnamese-style drip coffee (cà phê sữa đá, cà phê trứng/egg coffee if offered), fruit teas, smoothies, and espresso-based drinks, alongside pastries or light snacks — though exact prices and seasonal menu items are best confirmed on arrival or via the café's own social media, since these details shift over time. Budget roughly an hour to 90 minutes: enough time to order, find a view seat, and let the light change if you're there near sunset. Weekends and holiday periods bring larger crowds, especially domestic tourist groups, so arriving on a weekday morning or heading in during the mid-afternoon lull tends to mean shorter waits for the best-positioned tables.

## When to go

Late afternoon, roughly 4:30–6:30pm, is the sweet spot: the heat of the day has eased, and the light over the bay softens for photos. Ha Long Bay's climate follows a wet, hot summer (May–September, with occasional typhoon risk) and a cooler, drier, sometimes foggy winter (November–March), and both bookend seasons work for a café visit, though winter mornings can be misty enough to mute the view. If your main goal is the bay-view photo, avoid heavy midday sun, which tends to wash out the scene and push most seating into direct glare.

## Nearby & tips

Bãi Cháy is compact enough to combine easily: Bãi Cháy Beach and the Ha Long night market are both within a short walk or quick ride, and the Sun World Ha Long cable car and Queen Ferris wheel — one of the region's signature modern attractions — are a few minutes away by taxi. Many visitors stack La Long Coffee Hạ Long into a half-day loop with a boat tour departure point, a walk along the waterfront promenade, and dinner at one of the seafood restaurants clustered nearby. Because it's a popular stop with a high review count, it's worth checking recent photos or posts before visiting to see current interior layout and any seasonal changes, since cafés in fast-growing tourist zones like this one tend to refresh decor and menus more often than historic sites do.
