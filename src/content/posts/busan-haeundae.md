## What to know

Haeundae Beach is Busan's signature stretch of coastline — a broad, curving beach that anchors one of the city's busiest resort neighborhoods. It's known for its combination of city and sea: a dense skyline of hotels and apartment towers rising directly behind the sand, with cafés, seafood restaurants, and shops filling the streets just inland. In warmer months the beach draws large crowds for swimming and sunbathing, while in the off-season it's a popular spot for walking, watching the sunrise, and simply enjoying the sea air without the summer density.

Beyond the beach itself, the wider Haeundae area includes a seaside promenade that connects toward Dongbaek Island (a small forested headland with a walking path around its perimeter) and, further along the coast, the Dalmaji-gil hill area, known for its cherry blossoms in spring and scenic sea views. The Blueline Park coastal train and skywalk sit further east toward Songjeong, offering an easy way to see more of the coastline beyond Haeundae itself.

## How to get there

Haeundae is well served by the Busan subway — Haeundae Station on Line 2 puts you within walking distance of the beach and main street. City buses also run frequently to the area from other parts of Busan, and it's a common taxi destination if you're coming from Busan Station or Gimhae Airport. Because it's one of the city's major hubs, signage and transit connections are generally straightforward for visitors unfamiliar with Korean.

## Tips

- **Best times to visit:** Early morning or evening avoids the heaviest crowds, especially in peak summer. Spring and autumn offer comfortable weather for walking the promenade without beach-season congestion.
- **Combine with nearby spots:** Gwangalli Beach (with its view of Gwangan Bridge) and Songjeong Beach are both a short trip away, making it easy to build a coastal day out around Haeundae.
- **Dining:** The streets behind the beach are packed with restaurants, cafés, and street food stalls, with a strong emphasis on seafood given the coastal setting — worth wandering rather than rushing to one specific place.
- **Water and beach conditions:** If planning to swim, check the season, as official swimming periods and any safety flags can vary; conditions change outside the designated summer swimming window.
- **Walking options:** The Dongbaek Island trail and Dalmaji hill road are good low-effort walks if you want sea views away from the main crowded stretch of sand.
- **Practicalities:** As with most beach destinations, bring cash for small vendors, sun protection in summer, and expect the area to be significantly busier on weekends and holidays.

Haeundae works well both as a standalone half-day visit and as a base for exploring Busan's eastern coastline.
